Humanities and Technology A new engineering course at the UTC

Humanities and Technology is a new engineering course at the University of Technology of Compiègne. It is simultaneously opened to scientific students, economics students and literary students. Indeed, one of the aims is to connect consistently sciences, maths, philosophy and human sciences in the engineer training.


The Technologies and Human Sciences Department pushes forward the following idea: "Invent the technology also means invent the human beings." In other words, be part of the technological world means that we have to understand how technologies work, but also the stakes and impacts on societies. Humanities and Technology falls within this approach and is aimed at training engineers who are able to think globally about the Human-Technologies interactions. As an innovation, the course deserves a follow-up, readjustments, discussions and the students have had the chance to participate.


"Conceive, innovate a product or a technology means find the human aspects in the technique, find the technical aspects in human beings and that way participate in building of Humanity" (N.Salzmann, UTC).





  • A three-year course at UTC.
  • New approach to the engineering.
  • Philosophy, Epistemology, history, innovation, economics, languages, computer science, logic, mathematics, algorithms.
  • Learn more about Humanities and Technology

From industrial capitalism to cognitive capitalism Seminary Philosophy, Technology and Cognition at UTC

From industrial capitalism to cognitive capitalism is a dissertation that was written within the seminary PHITECO (Philosophy, Technology and Cognition) at the University of Technology of Compiègne, in Spring 2014.


The aim of was to express some faults of the industrial capitalism and then to introduce the new model of the cognitive capitalism (which was theorized by the economist Yann Moulier-Boutang). Cognitive capitalism is a new form of capitalism in which the concept of contribution (the production of knowledge by men and for men) is highly enhanced.


This project was highly inspired of the books L'abeille et l'économiste and Le capitalisme cognitif: la nouvelle grande transformation by Yann Moulier-Boutang.





  • From industrial capitalism to cognitive capitalism.
  • Seminary Philosophy, Technology and Cognition.
  • Spring 2014.

Methodology of the contemporary engineer

Methodology of the contemporary engineer is a project realized with the help of 7 other students, in Autumn 2013.


The aim was to establish a general methodology of the contemporary engineer. How is the engineer suppose to act in contemporary organisations? What competencies has he to mobilize in order to put a project through? What methodological tools does he use to reply to an order and to guide a team of experts in research of solutions?


The contemporary engineer is a technologist who can think about human stakes and impacts of technologies. He's also a methodologist whose expertise is defined in terms of questioning. He's not a scientist anymore, who just applies his knowledge but a player of the actual complexity. Finally, he is a leader, a pedagogue who knows how to put labor groups in innovation states leading to projects success.





  • Methodology of the contemporary engineer.
  • Team of 8 students at at UTC.
  • Autumn 2013.
  • Link to the book of Hugues Choplin, contemporary engineer and philosopher.

Reinvent pedagogy in the MOOCs movement Value analysis

Invent again pedagogy in the MOOCs movement is a project that was realized within a course about value analysis, in autumn 2013.


The aim was to understand and to analyse the impact of the MOOCs (Massive Online Open Courses) on pedagogy and university education, and then to suggest alternative ideas.


In the digital age, the MOOCs come within a movement particularly interesting and logical from an educational perspective. However, they have faults from a pedagogical perspective. What are the advantages and the disadvantages of such a technological innovation? How can we use the revolution of the MOOCs in order to improve pedagogy without destroying our current system? In other words, how can we use the contribution of the MOOCs and simultaneously optimize the students upbringing?





  • Reinvent pedagogy in the MOOCs movement.
  • Team of 6 students at UTC.
  • Autumn 2013.
  • School project.
  • Read the article by Dominique Boulier, sociology teacher at Sciences Po.

From theatres architecture to spectators History of Techniques

From theatres architecture to spectators is a disertation, which was written within a course about history of techniques, in autumn 2012.


The aim was to show that the architecture of theatres is a fundamental aspect to understand interactions between societies and performing arts. How are both worlds connected? How has the architecture of theatres revealed its social role over the years? Actually, the architecture of theatres is the result of the evolution of mentalities in societies and is the response to political and economical states.

  • History of techniques.
  • Autumn 2012.
